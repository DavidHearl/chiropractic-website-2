FAQs

What is chiropractic?

Chiropractors are professionals who specialise in the diagnosis and treatment of painful conditions due to mechanical dysfunction of the joints and soft tissue.

Chiropractors use their hands to adjust (manipulate) the joints and soft tissue of your spine and limbs where signs of restriction in movement are found, improving mobility and relieving pain.

What conditions do chiropractors treat?

Although chiropractors are best known for treating back and neck pain, which they do very well, patients also consult chiropractors regarding a range of other, related conditions including:

  • Lower back pain and Sciatica
  • Neck pain
  • Whiplash
  • Migraine and headaches
  • Shoulder and arm pain
  • Hip and knee complaints

Will treatment hurt?

Adjusting is not painful. If you have acute muscle spasms or resist the adjustment there may be some discomfort.

What is the popping noise of adjustments?

When the surfaces of a joint are moved apart rapidly, as happens in a chiropractic adjustment, there is a change of pressure within the joint space. This may cause a miniscule bubble of gas to pop. This sound is not significant and does not hurt.

Do I need GP referal?

No, chiropractors are primary healthcare professionals, so you can consult us directly.

Can I get treatment on the NHS?

Some GP's are able to purchase treatment for their patients on the NHS. Most health insurance companies will now pay for treatment, although some might require a letter from your GP. If you are looking to claim back for the treatment, it is best to check your policy.

How long does a treatment take?

The first consultation takes about 60 minutes. This includes a full health consultation and assessment. After your initial consultation treatment is based on your specific condition, so your appointment can last anything from 10 to 30 minutes.

What should I wear?

Loose comfortable clothes.

  • Treatment £35

Can I pay by card?

Yes, you can pay by cash or card.

Attending your appointment

Appointments are staggered. This is to reduce the chance of people crossing over when arriving or leaving.

Please aim to arrive at your appointment time. Wait in your car, I will come out to the door and wave you in. I will be wearing scrubs, a disposable apron, a face mask and gloves.

You should attend your appointment alone where possible.

A box will be provided for you to put your belongings in. If possible, please leave them in your car.

If you walk or arrive by public transport the waiting area will be open as normal. Seats have been spread out the ensure anyone using them will be 2 metres apart.

If you have a face mask please wear it.

Please bring a hand towel with you. You can use this to rest your face on if you are lying on your front during your treatment.

Inside IMSL House

Although it is not possible to maintain a 2 metre distance from me during your treatment, please maintain a 2 metre distance from other users of IMSL House.

If possible, try not to touch anything inside the building. The IMSL House door will be left unlocked so you won't have to use the buzzer or touch the door handle - you can push the door open with your shoulder.

Use hand gel to sanitise your hands when entering and leaving the building.

Please use the toilet before leaving home. However, if you do have to use the toilet whilst at your appointment, please let me know so I can follow the correct cleaning protocol.
